When a company is on the brink of administration, directors often wait too long to act.

By then, the bank or creditors have already moved, appointing their own administrator.

Directors lose control, their business is broken up, and they risk wrongful trading claims, misfeasance actions, or personal guarantees being enforced.

 Without early intervention, directors are left exposed and powerless.

The Solution

Emqore steps in pre-appointment to protect directors and preserve value.

We install a rescue-minded administrator from our coalition, giving directors influence over the process before it’s too late.

We provide legal firepower to shield directors personally while structuring survival strategies such as pre-pack sales, CVAs, or restructuring plans.

In short, we support directors, conserve the business, and stop hostile creditors from dictating the outcome.

The Result

With Emqore, directors and companies help shape their own insolvency, stay onside with creditors, and take back control of their cash-flows.

Unsecured and minority creditors almost always lose out during insolvency:

The average recovery is just ~1.48 pence for every £1 owed.

In many liquidations, the median recovery is 0%. Nothing at all.

Administrators are appointed to act for all creditors, but without pressure, they may prioritise speed and simplicity over maximum recoveries.

The Solution

When creditors unite, they can change outcomes.

Emqore brings together dispersed creditors (small, unsecured, or minority) into blocs and forms committees of 3–5 members to give them a formal voice.

We run the committee with the legal firepower needed to hold administrators to account, and maximise debt recovery.

That means pursuing claims against directors, clawing back undervalued sales, and challenging unfair preferences to best support creditors.

The Result

The result is simple: better recoveries, such as turning a 2p/£ distribution into 5p/£ or more.

And because our fees are outcome-based, you pay us nothing upfront. When we deliver that uplift, we seek to attain remuneration from the debtors’ estate.
